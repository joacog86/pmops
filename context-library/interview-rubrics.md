# Interview Rubrics by Company

Load this file when running `/interview-prep` or mock interviews.

## Google — PM Interview Style

**Philosophy:** Structure first, user empathy second, data always.
Google PMs are expected to think in frameworks, name tradeoffs explicitly, and define metrics before diving into solutions.

**Common question types:**
- "Design a product for X" (Product Sense)
- "A metric dropped — how do you investigate?" (Execution)
- "Should Google enter market X?" (Strategy)
- "Tell me about a time you influenced without authority" (Behavioral)

**What they're looking for:**
| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| User empathy | You named specific user segments and their actual pain, not abstract "users" |
| Framework | You used a clear structure (CIRCLES, DIGS, or equivalent) without being robotic |
| Metrics | You defined success metrics before proposing solutions |
| Tradeoffs | You explicitly said what you're NOT doing and why |
| Communication | You signposted your structure out loud: "I'm going to break this into 3 parts..." |

**Red flags at Google:**
- No user segmentation (jumping straight to solutions)
- Vague success metrics ("engagement" without defining it)
- Feature lists without prioritization logic
- Not asking clarifying questions before diving in

**Scoring bar:** Senior PM = 4+/5 on all dimensions. Staff PM = 5/5 on structure and tradeoffs.

## Meta — PM Interview Style

**Philosophy:** Move fast, think at scale, data is the answer.
Meta PMs are growth-oriented. They want to see you think about impact at billions-of-users scale, use data instinctively, and make fast opinionated calls.

**Common question types:**
- "How would you improve Facebook Groups?" (Product Sense)
- "DAU dropped 10% — what do you do?" (Execution)
- "Should Meta enter X?" (Strategy)
- "Tell me about a product decision you made with incomplete data" (Behavioral)

**What they're looking for:**
| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| Growth mindset | You think about network effects, virality, retention loops — not just features |
| Data instinct | You immediately ask for data, define what you'd measure, think in percentages |
| Impact at scale | Your solutions consider what happens at 1B users |
| Speed of reasoning | You get to a recommendation fast. No over-hedging. |
| Execution clarity | You can break your idea into specific eng deliverables |

**Red flags at Meta:**
- Solutions that only work at small scale
- Qualitative reasoning without any numbers
- Over-engineering: too much process, not enough output
- "It depends" answers without a final recommendation

**Scoring bar:** Clear recommendation in first 2 minutes. Numbers in every answer.

## Anthropic — PM Interview Style

**Philosophy:** First principles, mission alignment, AI-native thinking.
Anthropic PMs need technical depth, genuine values alignment with AI safety, and the ability to think about product in an AI-first way — not bolt-on AI.

**Common question types:**
- "How would you improve Claude for developers?" (Product Sense)
- "What metrics would you use for a responsible AI product?" (Execution)
- "Should we build X feature?" (Strategy with ethical dimension)
- "Tell me about a time you navigated a values conflict in product decisions" (Behavioral)

**What they're looking for:**
| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| AI product intuition | You understand what makes AI products different: latency, hallucination, context, evaluation |
| First principles | You question assumptions, re-derive answers rather than apply templates |
| Technical depth | You understand how LLMs work well enough to reason about product tradeoffs |
| Mission alignment | You genuinely care about beneficial AI — not just as talking point |
| Nuanced tradeoffs | You see safety vs. capability, helpfulness vs. harm — and reason through them clearly |

**Red flags at Anthropic:**
- Treating AI as just another feature to bolt on
- No awareness of failure modes (hallucination, misuse, dual use)
- Generic PM frameworks without adapting to AI context
- Mission-washing: performing values without depth

**Scoring bar:** Technical fluency + values coherence. They'll probe hard on both.

## Cross-company: What Every PM Lead Interview Rewards

Regardless of company, these are universal signals for PM Lead level:

1. **Opinionated** — You make a call. You don't hide behind "it depends."
2. **User-specific** — You name the user. Not "users" — "a 32-year-old urban commuter who..."
3. **Hypothesis-driven** — You form a hypothesis before diving into data
4. **Tradeoff-aware** — You name what you're giving up and why it's worth it
5. **Outcome-focused** — You connect features to business outcomes, not just user delight
6. **Self-aware** — In behavioral, you know what you'd do differently
